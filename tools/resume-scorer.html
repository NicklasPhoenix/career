Resume Best Practices

Follow these guidelines to create a strong, effective resume:

Keep It Concise

One page for entry-level candidates, two pages for experienced professionals. Recruiters don't have time to read lengthy resumes.

Use Action Verbs

Start bullet points with strong action verbs like "managed," "developed," "implemented," "increased," and "led."

Quantify Achievements

Include specific numbers to demonstrate your impact: "Increased website traffic by 45%" rather than "Improved website traffic."

Tailor for Each Application

Customize your resume for each job by incorporating keywords and requirements from the job description.

Focus on Results

Emphasize accomplishments rather than just listing responsibilities. Show how you made a difference in your previous roles.

Use a Clean Format

Choose a professional, easy-to-read format with consistent formatting, adequate white space, and clear section headings.

Include Relevant Keywords

Use industry-specific terms and skills that applicant tracking systems and recruiters will be searching for.

Common Resume Mistakes to Avoid

Steer clear of these frequent errors that can hurt your job search:

Generic Resumes

Using the same resume for every application reduces your chances of getting noticed. Tailor your resume to highlight the most relevant experience for each position.

Vague Language

Avoid phrases like "responsible for" or "duties included." Instead, use specific, action-oriented language that demonstrates your contributions.

Too Much Text

Dense paragraphs are difficult to scan. Use bullet points to break up information and make key points stand out.

Irrelevant Information

Include only experience and skills that are relevant to the jobs you're targeting. For experienced professionals, it's not necessary to include every job from the past 20 years.

Spelling and Grammar Errors

Even small errors can create a negative impression. Proofread carefully and consider having someone else review your resume.

Unprofessional Email Address

Use a simple email address with your name rather than playful or unprofessional handles.

Photos and Graphics

In most industries, photos and elaborate graphics are not appropriate and can distract from your qualifications.

How to Improve Your Resume Score

Use these strategies to boost your resume's effectiveness:

Conduct a Keyword Audit

Analyze job descriptions in your target field and identify frequently mentioned skills and qualifications. Incorporate these keywords naturally into your resume.

Transform Responsibilities into Achievements

For each position, think about specific accomplishments and quantify them with numbers, percentages, or timeframes.

Optimize Your Summary Statement

Replace an objective statement with a professional summary that highlights your key qualifications and value proposition.

Use the PAR Method

Structure bullet points using Problem-Action-Result format: describe a challenge, the action you took, and the positive result.

Get Feedback

Ask trusted colleagues, mentors, or career counselors to review your resume and provide constructive feedback.

Test with ATS Simulators

Use free online tools to see how your resume appears when parsed by applicant tracking systems.

When to Get a Professional Resume Review

Consider investing in a professional resume writer if:

  • You're making a significant career change
  • You've been unemployed for an extended period
  • You're targeting executive-level positions
  • You've been applying for jobs without getting interviews
  • You're returning to the workforce after a break
  • You're in a highly competitive field

Professional resume writers have expertise in industry-specific best practices and can help you present your experience in the most compelling way.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the resume scorer?

Our resume scorer uses algorithms based on hiring manager preferences and applicant tracking system requirements. While it provides valuable feedback, it's not a substitute for human review. Use the score as a guide to identify areas for improvement, but also seek feedback from professionals in your field.

What's a good resume score?

Scores of 80-100 indicate a strong resume with minor areas for improvement. Scores of 60-79 suggest a decent resume that needs significant optimization. Scores below 60 indicate major issues that should be addressed before applying for jobs. Remember that scores are relative and should be used to track improvement over time.
